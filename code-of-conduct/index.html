Code of conduct

How we keep the conversation useful.

Nirman Darpan succeeds or fails on the quality of the comments, votes and accountability records on it. The rules below exist to keep them useful — for residents, for journalists, for officials reading honest feedback.

Three rules for comments

  1. Be specific. "The work is slow" is noise. "The retaining wall at km 47 of NH-5 collapsed during the 22 July monsoon and hasn't been repaired" is signal. Names, dates, locations, numbers.
  2. Be civil. Argue with the project, with the data, with the decision, with the contractor — never with another commenter. No slurs, no threats, no doxxing, no harassment.
  3. Be sourced where possible. If you cite a specific event — an accident, an FIR, a delayed payment — link to the source if you can. We don't require sources on opinions; we do on factual claims.

Rules for accountability submissions

The accountability module (Incidents · Defects · Audits · Grievances · Litigation) is held to a stricter standard because those entries become part of the public record:

  • A source URL is required on every submission. News article, RTI reply, audit PDF, court order — something independently verifiable.
  • Every submission lands as pending review. A moderator verifies before it is promoted to verified and displayed without the pending tag.
  • Once an entry is promoted to verified, you cannot retract it. The right of reply (below) is the way to add context.
  • Severity ratings (low / medium / high) should match the severity reported in the source, not your personal sense of it.

What gets removed

Comments and submissions may be removed if they:

  • Are obvious spam (advertising, link drops, repeated copy-paste).
  • Contain slurs, threats, harassment, or doxxing.
  • Are off-topic for the project they're attached to.
  • Make a factual claim that is demonstrably false and the commenter does not correct it after one notice.
  • Are part of a coordinated campaign (e.g. multiple new accounts posting near-identical copy in a short window).
  • Violate Indian law (defamation that is actually actionable, content prohibited under IT Act 2000 § 69A, etc.).

Right of reply

If you are an official, a contractor, or an organisation named in any project record or accountability entry, you can submit a response that is published next to the entry it concerns.

  1. Write to ravikntsh@gmail.com with: the project ID, the entry you are responding to, your organisational identity, and your reply.
  2. We verify the identity (one round of email confirmation against an organisation-level address, usually).
  3. The reply publishes alongside the entry without editing for tone. We may add a one-line editorial note giving context (e.g., "Reply received 21 July; concerns the audit observation dated 12 June").
  4. Replies are public. If you want to send a confidential correction, use the corrections process instead.

Appeals

If a comment of yours is removed and you think we got it wrong, you can appeal:

  • Single appeal per moderation action.
  • Email ravikntsh@gmail.com with the comment text and the reason you believe it should be restored.
  • Reviewed within 7 days. If restored, that fact is logged on the corrections page. If upheld, you get one-paragraph reasoning back.

One last principle

The point of all this is not to police speech — it is to keep the signal-to-noise ratio high enough that a busy resident, a busy journalist, or a busy official can come here and find something useful in 90 seconds. Every rule above exists in service of that.

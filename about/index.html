About

An independent tracker for public works in Himachal Pradesh.

Nirman Darpan brings together — in one place, for one state — the records, status, budgets, accountability flags and public conversation around every major infrastructure project in Himachal Pradesh.

Why this exists

Information about Himachal's public works is scattered. The status of a single highway lives across the NHAI project portal, two press archives, an RTI reply on Twitter and a YouTube comment from a villager whose road got cut off. A citizen asking "what's happening with NH-5?" has to assemble that from twelve places — assuming they know where to look.

Nirman Darpan brings those records into one citation-backed view. Every project page collects what's known, links to where it came from, and (when verified residents sign in) adds their voices and accountability flags alongside.

What we cover

The current dataset spans 32 projects across all 12 districts of Himachal Pradesh — highways and tunnels, hydropower and solar, hospitals and medical colleges, rail, smart-city schemes, water supply, schools, and disaster restoration after the 2023 floods. The roster grows as new projects come online and as we encounter ones worth tracking.

What this is not

  • Not affiliated with the Government of Himachal Pradesh, any political party, or any contractor.
  • Not anonymous. Comments, votes and accountability submissions require verified resident sign-in — see the code of conduct.
  • Not paid. No advertising, no government grant, no contractor sponsorship — see funding.
  • Not a news site. We aggregate and link; we don't repost press content. See methodology.

Who built it

Nirman Darpan is built and maintained by Ravi Sharma, an independent technologist and Himachal resident, working with the AI development tool Claude (Anthropic) as a coding collaborator. All editorial decisions — what to track, what to verify, what to publish — are made by Ravi.

Contact: ravikntsh@gmail.com.

Open source, MIT licensed

The full source code, the data files, the curation workflow and the schema are public at github.com/Raviknight/nirman-darpan under the MIT license. Anyone can audit, fork, extend, or replicate the model for another state. The git commit history serves as the project's audit log — every change to any project record is permanently traceable.

Get involved

  • As a resident: sign in on the main tracker and add your voice on any project — votes, comments, or accountability flags.
  • As a journalist: happy to walk through the methodology, share findings, or surface an under-covered story. Email above.
  • As a civic technologist: open a pull request, file an issue, or fork the codebase for your state.
  • As an official or contractor: see the right-of-reply section. Responses to any accountability entry are published alongside the original.

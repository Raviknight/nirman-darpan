Funding

Who pays for this, and what they get for it.

A civic-transparency project is only as credible as the answers to those two questions.

Current funding (as of 19 June 2026)

100% self-funded by Ravi Sharma.
No advertising. No government grant. No contractor sponsorship. No NGO funding. Operating cost so far: zero per month, except a domain registration when we buy one (~₹700/year).

What we will not accept

  • Advertising — would compromise editorial neutrality.
  • Sponsorship from any contractor named on the site — direct conflict of interest.
  • Sponsorship from any government department whose projects we track — direct conflict of interest.
  • Sponsorship from political parties — direct conflict of interest.

What we would consider

  • Small reader contributions to offset domain + future hosting upgrades. If we ever accept these, the total raised and what it covered will be published here annually.
  • Grants from civic-tech or press-freedom foundations (e.g. Internews, Mozilla, Sunlight Foundation) that come with no editorial strings. Each grant accepted will be disclosed here within 30 days.
  • Partnerships with universities or civic-tech labs that contribute volunteer time. Listed publicly.

How to verify our independence

  • The full source code, the data, and the curation workflow are public at github.com/Raviknight/nirman-darpan. Anyone can audit what we publish and how.
  • If a funding source ever materially shapes editorial decisions (e.g., influences whether a project gets covered or how its accountability flags are described), it would show up in commit history and reviewer comments.
  • Email ravikntsh@gmail.com with any specific question.
